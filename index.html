Did You Know? Surprising findings from the research
💡Did You Know?

Lavender is the most studied scent — appearing in 7+ studies. It consistently increases theta and alpha waves, inducing measurable drowsiness even in infants of depressed mothers!

💡Did You Know?

Your nose detects odors even when your brain doesn't! Galaxolide produced divided attention effects even when subjects couldn't consciously detect the scent.

💡Did You Know?

Mirror-image molecules have opposite effects. The (R) and (S) forms of linalool are near-identical, yet one creates a favorable brain impression while the other is unfavorable!

💡Did You Know?

Women's brains are more sensitive to odors. Exposed to identical fragrances, women showed consistently stronger EEG changes — suggesting a biological difference in olfactory processing.

💡Did You Know?

Professional perfumers have different brains! Expert perfumers respond to odors mainly in the frontal region — unlike general workers whose responses are more diffuse.

💡Did You Know?

Food odors activate reward circuits. Strawberry aroma triggered reward brain regions, while lily of the valley activated entirely different neural pathways — same nose, different brain!

α
🔵 Alpha (8–13 Hz)

Relaxed Awareness

Increased by lavender, neroli, rose. Decreased by rosemary — signals active engagement.

β
🟠 Beta (13–30 Hz)

Active Thinking

Jasmine increases beta in the left frontal region. Linked to mood elevation and well-being.

θ
🟢 Theta (4–8 Hz)

Deep Calm

Birch tar, jasmine, lavender & lemon increase theta. Valerian produces large increases.

δ
🔴 Delta (0.5–4 Hz)

Deep Rest

Valerian increases delta. Rarely altered by aromatic stimuli alone — deepest rest state.

γ
🟡 Gamma (30+ Hz)

Peak Cognition

Mentha arvensis caused gamma to decrease, indicating stress relief. A frontier of study.

More Surprising Facts Science is stranger than fiction
💡Did You Know?

An unpleasant smell physically shuts down your cortex. Valeric acid caused alpha-2 increases — direct cortical deactivation, as if the brain literally withdraws from the bad smell.

💡Did You Know?

Culture shapes how your nose works. Sulphur compounds triggered different theta patterns across population groups — odor responses are culturally programmed into the brain.

💡Did You Know?

Sedative scents improve productivity! Lemon, lavender and sandalwood were linked to improved work output, while awakening scents were better at reducing perceived workload.

💡Did You Know?

Heated soybean smell changes brain waves. Soybeans heated in sugar-amino solution created aroma compounds that measurably increased alpha waves — a kitchen smell becoming neuroscience!

💡Did You Know?

Jasmine oil makes you feel romantic. Beta increases in anterior and left posterior regions triggered by jasmine were directly linked to feelings of well-being and romance.

💡Did You Know?

Jujube seed oil increases attention AND relaxation simultaneously! Fast alpha increased in THREE prefrontal/frontal regions at once — a rare dual-state almost unique to this oil.
